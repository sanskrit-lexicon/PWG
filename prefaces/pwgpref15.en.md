# Foreword

The second volume of the dictionary, which we herewith present to the public, has been brought to completion in a shorter span of time than the first, even though it contains almost as many printed sheets as the latter. The material has meanwhile increased considerably, as every attentive user of the dictionary will at once perceive. Not only the editors of the dictionary themselves, but also their friend Weber, have year by year enlarged the circle of literary works drawn upon at the beginning of the work; and from the letter ज onward we have moreover received from Dr. Kern in Groenlo, an able pupil of Weber's, very valuable contributions from the works of Varâhamihira, which until now exist only in manuscript.

We originally intended to complete the gutturals, palatals, and so-called cerebrals in the second volume; but the concern that the second volume would thereby become considerably larger than the first, and that its use would be made still more difficult for some time, has determined us to close with the letter ट. The third volume will, so far as one can foresee, bring the dentals to completion and thus conclude the half of the whole work.

St. Petersburg /
Tübingen, 14/26 October 1858.
