# Explanation of the Abbreviations.

(Printed works from Sanskrit literature that are cited only very occasionally are marked with an asterisk.)

**Âśv. Śr.** = Âçvalâjana's Çrautasûtrâni in 12 Adhjâja. Manuscript.
**Âçv. Gṛhj.** = Âçvalâjana's Gṛhjasûtrâni in 4 Adhjâja. Ms.
**Adbh. Br.** = Adbhutabrâhmaṇa.
**Adbhutas.** = Adbhutasâra.
**A Dict. Beng. and S.** = A Dictionary Bengali and Sanskrit, explained in English.
**Agnisv.** = Agnisvâmin, a scholiast of the Lâṭjâjana.
**Âhnikat.** = Âhnikatattva.
**Ait. Br.** = Aitarejabrâhmaṇa. Cited according to the 8 Pańḱikâ (an external division, into which the 40 Adhjâja formed on factual considerations are split) and the chapter numbers running continuously within the Pańḱikâ. Ms. Sâjana's Commentary thereto. Ms.
**Ait. Up.** = Aitarejopanishad, according to the edition by Röer in the Bibliotheca indica.
**AK.** = Amarakosha according to the edition by Colebrooke and Loiseleur Deslongchamps (Gild. Bibl. 251. 253).
**Amar.** = Amaruçataka according to the Calc. edition (Gild. Bibl. 162).
**Amarad.** = Amaradatta.
**Amṛtav. Up.** = Amṛtavindûpanishad.
**an.** = Anekârthasaṃgraha (see H. an.).
**Ânandal.** = Ânandalaharî in Haeb. Chrest. p. 246. ff.
**Ânandav.** = Ânandavallî.
**Anekârthadhv.** = Anekârthadhvanimańḱarî, a dictionary; according to the ÇKDr.
**Anh.** = Appendix.
**Annaṃbh.** = Annaṃbhaṭṭa.
**Anukr.** = Anukramaṇikâ.
**Anup. S.** = Anupadasûtra.
**Âpast.** = Âpastamba.
**Arǵ.** = Arǵunasamâgama, ed. Bopp (Gild. Bibl. 106).
**Ârun. Up.** = Ârunikopanishad.
**As. Res.** = Asiatick Researches; or, transactions of the Society instituted in Bengal, for inquiring into the History and Antiquities, the Arts, Sciences, and Literature, of Asia. London.
**AV.** = Atharvavedasaṃhitâ, edited by R. Roth and W. D. Whitney. Berlin at F. Dümmler. 1855. 8°. In the first sheets of the dictionary there are several citations whose numbers do not quite agree with the numbering adopted in the edition. The difference can be reconciled by counting, in the composite hymns (Parjâjasûkta) which are counted as units in the edition, the subdivisions (strophes) as separate hymns.
**Avad. Śat.** = Avadânaçataka, a Buddhist collection of legends.
**AV. Anukr.** = Anukramaṇikâ of the Atharvaveda, 10 Paṭala. Ms.
**avj.** = avjaja (see Med.).

**AV. Pariś.** = Pariçishṭa of the Atharvaveda.
**AV. Prât.** = Prâtiçâkhja to the Atharvaveda. Cited according to Adhjâja and Sûtra. Ms. See Roth's Introduction to the Nirukta, p. XLVII.
**Bâlab.** = Bâlabodhanî in Windischmann's Sancara (Gild. Bibl. 275).
**Benf. Chr.** = Benfey' Chrestomathie aus Sanskritwerken. Leipzig. 1853.
**Benf. Gr.** = Benfey, Vollständige Grammatik der Sanskritsprache. Leipzig 1852.
**Bhag.** = Bhagavadgîtâ, edition by Schlegel (Gild. Bibl. 116).
**Bhâg. P.** = Bhâgavatapurâṇa, according to citations in the VP. and ÇKDr. From ऋ onward the 9 Skandha edited by Burnouf (Gild. Bibl. 125) have been exploited.
**Bhânud.** = Bhânudîkshita, a commentator of the Amarakosha; according to citations in the ÇKDr.
**Bhar.** = Bharata, an author on dramatic art, distinct from the scholiast to the AK. (the latter according to citations in the ÇKDr.).
**Bhartṛ.** = Bhartṛhari, edition by Bohlen (Gild. Bibl. 156), with regard to the small work: Variae lectiones ad Bohlenii editionem Bhartriharis sententiarum pertinentes, e codicibus extractae per A. Schiefner et A. Weber. Berolini 1850.
**\*Bhâshâp.** = Bhâshâpariḱḱheda according to the edition by Röer in the Bibliotheca indica.
**\*Bhaṭṭ.** = Bhaṭṭikâvja, ed. Calc. (Gild. Bibl. 137).
**Bhâvapr.** = Bhâvaprakâça, a medical dictionary; according to citations in the ÇKDr.
**Bhav. P.** = Bhavishjapurâṇa.
**Bhûripr.** = Bhûriprajoga, a dictionary; according to citations in the ÇKDr.
**Brâhman.** = Brahmaṇavilâpa in Bopp's Ardschuna's Reise zu Indra's Himmel etc. Berlin 1824.
**Brahmâṇḍa-P.** = Brahmâṇḍapurâṇa.
**Brahma-P.** = Brahmapurâṇa in Lassen's Anthologie (Gild. Bibl. 48).
**Brahmav. P.** = Brahmavaivartapurâṇa.
**\*Brahma-S.** = Brahmasûtra, according to citations in Wind. Sancara.
**Brahmop.** = Brahmopanishad.
**Bṛh. Âr. Up.** = Bṛhadâraṇjakopanishad, according to the edition by Röer in the Bibliotheca indica.
**Bṛh. Âr. Up. (Pol.)** = id., in the edition by Poley (Gild. Bibl. 78).
**Bṛhasp.** = Bṛhaspati, author of a law book; according to citations in Mit. and Dâj.
**Bṛh. Dev.** = Bṛhaddevatâ; see Roth, zur L. u. G. d. W. p. 49.
**Bull. hist.-phil.** = Bulletin historico-philologique de l'Académie Impériale des sciences de St.-Pétersbourg.
**Burn. Intr.** = Burnouf, Introduction à l'histoire du Buddhisme indien. Tome premier. Paris 1844.
**Burn. Lot. de la b. l.** = Le Lotus de la bonne loi, traduit du Sanscrit etc. par M. E. Burnouf. Paris 1852.
